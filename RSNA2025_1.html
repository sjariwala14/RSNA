Abstract

This comprehensive report captures key insights from executive interviews and competitive analyses conducted at RSNA 2025, highlighting transformative trends such as AI-driven opportunistic screening, enterprise imaging platforms, advanced visualization tools, and the strategic consolidation of AI solutions across the radiology landscape.

Emerging technologies—including photon-counting CT, helium-free MRI, and integrated AI-PACS systems—demonstrate a significant shift toward more efficient, personalized, and accessible diagnostic workflows. These innovations address critical challenges in modern healthcare delivery, including rising imaging volumes, radiologist burnout, and the need for precision medicine.

The findings underscore a transformative period in radiology, where AI integration and interoperability have become central to addressing operational inefficiencies, improving clinical outcomes, and optimizing diagnostic performance across healthcare systems globally.

A. RSNA 2025 — Interview with Riverain Technologies

Watch Full Interview: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tgmotaRiJes
Complete Summary

This interview with Dr. James Hillis of Mass General Brigham focuses on opportunistic cardiac calcium scoring with AI. The discussion centers on applying artificial intelligence to extract cardiovascular risk information from routine, non-cardiac-gated chest CT scans—a practice known as opportunistic screening.

Technical Approach: Riverain's AI software analyzes existing chest CT scans performed for other clinical indications (lung screening, trauma, etc.) to automatically calculate coronary artery calcium (CAC) scores without requiring additional radiation or specialized cardiac protocols.

Clinical Validation: A landmark study of nearly 500 cases demonstrated the AI achieved very high agreement (above 0.95) and correlation (above 0.97) with both expert radiologist readings and traditional gold-standard gated cardiac CT measurements. This validation was conducted across diverse patient populations and scanner types.

Implementation Strategy: The software integrates seamlessly into existing PACS workflows, automatically processing eligible studies and presenting results within the radiologist's normal reading environment. No additional worklist management or separate application launch is required.

Detailed Analysis

The Problem with Traditional CAC Scoring:

  • Requires dedicated, "gated" cardiac CT scans with specific protocols
  • Involves specialized technologist training and additional radiation exposure (1-3 mSv)
  • Manual analysis by technologists or radiologists is time-consuming (5-15 minutes per case)
  • Low adoption rates: Only 5-10% of eligible patients receive CAC screening despite strong evidence base
  • Limited insurance coverage creates financial barriers to widespread screening

The AI Solution - Opportunistic Screening:

  • Riverain's AI software can automatically calculate CAC scores from routine, non-gated chest CTs
  • Leverages existing imaging data without additional radiation or patient time
  • Processes studies in under 60 seconds with minimal computational requirements
  • Generates standardized reports with ASCVD risk scores and clinical recommendations
  • Supports both Agatston and volume-based scoring methodologies

Clinical Impact and Future Directions:

  • Could expand screening to 10-20x more patients who already get chest CTs for other reasons
  • Enables population health approaches to cardiovascular risk stratification
  • Future integration with EHR systems for automated risk communication and follow-up scheduling
  • Potential expansion to other opportunistic findings (liver fat, bone density, muscle mass)
  • Development of longitudinal tracking capabilities for monitoring disease progression

Market Implications: This approach represents a shift from "dedicated screening" to "intelligent data mining" of existing imaging, potentially creating new reimbursement models and changing how preventive care is delivered.

What is the role of AI quantitation of coronary plaque?

Session Summary

Presenter: Dr. Jonathon Leipsic, Professor of Radiology and Cardiology, University of British Columbia

Session Focus: Exploration of AI's potential role in quantifying coronary plaque composition and distribution for improved cardiovascular risk assessment.

Key Points:

  • Established Value of Cardiac CT: Well-documented utility for diagnosing coronary artery disease and guiding treatment decisions
  • AI's Future Role: Potential for AI to provide detailed quantitative analysis of plaque burden and characteristics
  • Clinical Goal: Move beyond simple stenosis detection to personalized treatment response tracking
  • Research Focus: Understanding plaque composition and distribution, especially in individuals with premature disease

Current Limitations in Assessment Tools:

  • Calcium (CAC) Score Limitations:
    • Can underpredict risk in diverse populations
    • Less informative for patients already on statins
    • Limited ability to assess non-calcified plaque
    • Poor correlation with plaque progression in some patient subsets
  • Vulnerable Plaque Challenge:
    • Identifying high-risk plaque features remains challenging
    • Plaques evolve dynamically over time
    • Not all high-risk lesions lead to clinical events
    • Limited ability to predict individual patient risk

Clinical Problem: Many heart attack patients had no prior symptoms, underscoring the need to extract more predictive information from existing imaging studies.

AI Quantitation Potential and Challenges

AI's Potential Contributions:

  • Detailed Plaque Analysis: Comprehensive quantification of plaque burden, composition, and distribution
  • Risk Stratification: More nuanced, personalized approach to cardiovascular risk assessment
  • Treatment Monitoring: Potential to track plaque progression/regression in response to therapy
  • Research Applications: Enable large-scale studies of plaque biology and treatment effects

Technical Challenges:

  • Data Standardization: Variability in CT protocols across institutions and scanner types
  • Validation Requirements: Need for large, diverse datasets with clinical outcomes
  • Integration Complexity: Challenges in incorporating AI outputs into clinical workflow
  • Interpretation Standardization: Lack of consensus on clinically meaningful plaque metrics

Implementation Considerations:

  • Clinical Utility Evidence: Need for studies demonstrating improved patient outcomes
  • Workflow Integration: Seamless incorporation into existing reading environments
  • Regulatory Pathway: FDA clearance requirements for quantitative imaging biomarkers
  • Reimbursement Strategy: Payer acceptance of new AI-enhanced diagnostic codes

Current Status: Dr. Leipsic emphasized that while the technology shows significant promise, there remains substantial debate about when and how to implement AI-driven plaque quantitation in clinical practice, particularly for younger patients. The technology is not yet ready for routine use in tracking individual treatment response but represents an important area for continued research and development.

AI tool for breast ultrasound helps avoid unnecessary biopsies:

  • Presented by: Michael Fishman, MD, Mass General Brigham
  • Key Finding: AI-based decision support could reduce unnecessary biopsies of benign breast lesions by approximately 60%
  • Study Details: Prospective study of 107 biopsies in 102 women using FDA-cleared Koios DS AI tool
  • Results: AI correctly classified 96% (24/25) of malignant lesions as BI-RADS 4 and assigned lower-risk scores to 68.3% (56/82) of benign lesions
  • Impact: Retrospective analysis suggested 68% of benign biopsies could have been avoided at cost of potentially missing one cancer

3D CNN model estimates MACE risk from chest CT:

  • Technology: 3D convolutional neural network analyzing routine non-gated chest CT scans
  • Objective: Estimate 5-year risk of major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE)
  • Validation: Trained on 4,431 CTs from Mayo Clinic, validated on 201 CTs from Emory University
  • Performance: Outperformed traditional PREVENT scores and AI-based CAC scoring, especially in diverse populations
  • Future: Development of multimodal models combining imaging with clinical data and reports

Using AI with CT to assess epicardial fat predicts heart disease risk:

  • Presented by: Jong Eun Lee, MD, PhD, Asan Medical Center, Seoul
  • Study: Analysis of 5,342 asymptomatic adults with low-dose CT screening over 7.5 years
  • Finding: AI-measured epicardial adipose tissue volume independently predicts MACE risk beyond traditional factors
  • Clinical Impact: Provides additional risk stratification beyond coronary calcium scoring
  • Application: Could improve early identification of at-risk individuals for preventive interventions

LLMs perform well for annotating radiology reports:

  • Method: Standardized prompt framework tested across six major U.S. institutions
  • Objective: Automate extraction of diagnoses from radiology reports in real-world settings
  • Result: High consistency and effectiveness across different institutions and pathologies
  • Significance: Enables scalable data annotation for AI training and clinical research
  • Future Work: Testing with diverse report formats and improved generalizability

DICOM format 'ideal' for PACS in high-volume clinical settings:

  • Comparison: Secondary Capture vs. Grayscale Softcopy Presentation State (GSPS) for AI output storage
  • Finding: GSPS reduces storage requirements by nearly 50% for X-ray with similar savings across modalities
  • Benefits: Faster network transfer, avoids duplicate images, preserves original diagnostic quality
  • Recommendation: GSPS format ideal for high-volume clinical settings integrating AI

Integrated AI-PACS lightens chest imaging load:

  • System: Seamless integration minimizing workflow disruption for chest X-ray and CT
  • Performance: Average processing time of 1 minute for X-rays, 2 minutes for CT scans
  • Results: Positive radiologist feedback, enhanced triage of critical findings
  • Architecture: Successful integration of AI outputs directly into existing PACS interface
  • Significance: Practical model for deploying AI in clinical settings with reduced diagnosis time
